A proposition is a statement (declarative sentence) that is either true
or false, but not both.

Two statements are said to be logically equivalent, ≡, iff they have
identical truth values for each possible value of their statement
variables. (Corresponds to = with numbers)


Distributivity: (p ∨ (q ∧ r )) ≡ ((p ∨ q) ∧ (p ∨ r )),
(p ∧ (q ∨ r )) ≡ ((p ∧ q) ∨ (p ∧ r )),
p → q ≡ (¬p ∨ q)
De Morgan’s Laws:
¬(p ∨ q) ≡ ((¬p) ∧ (¬q)), ¬(p ∧ q) ≡ ((¬p) ∨ (¬q))

The negation of a conditional statement:
¬(p → q) ≡ p ∧ ¬q
